# Impeccable — Project Design Context

> Source of truth for design decisions on j4ck.xyz. Read this before any design work.
>
> **2026-06-14 — DIRECTION CHANGE.** The site is moving *away* from the terminal/hacker
> aesthetic (shell prompt, `SYS_STATUS`, monospace-everything) toward a refined, editorial
> **personal landing page**. The old "refined terminal" brief is retired. See below.

## Design Context

### Owner & privacy (READ FIRST)
This is jack's own personal site. **Refer to him only as `jack` or the handle `@j4ck.xyz`**
(his identity across the ATmosphere / Bluesky apps). **Never display a real or full name,
email address beyond an intentional contact link, location, or any other personal information.**
It's his own site, so it does not need to introduce or over-explain who he is.

### Users
People who land here to **find jack online and see what he makes** — his socials, his Bluesky
posts, his photography (Grain / Flashes), his writing (Leaflet), and his code (GitHub). It's a
hub and a calling card. It should feel like *a real person's well-made corner of the internet*:
confident, current, and unmistakably his — something a peer, a follower, or someone considering
working with him comes away impressed by.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **confident, editorial, personal.** Modern and self-assured without shouting.
The previous version read as "hacker terminal theme"; the new one should read as *"a designer/
builder with taste made this."* Personality through typography, composition, and a single
decisive red — not through system chrome or gimmicks.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Editorial personal landing page — red & black, professional.**

- **Structure:** A **hero + scrolling sections** home page (not a dashboard, not a grid of
  identical tiles). A strong intro hero (the `j4ck.xyz` / `jack` wordmark + a short, human bio
  line), then sections you scroll through: latest posts, a photo strip, links, code. The
  sub-pages (posts, photos, blog, repos) keep working as today; only the look changes.
- **Theme:** Dark. Near-black canvas. Black is the field; red is the gesture.
- **Color:** Red is the brand and stays — but **refine it from the current hot neon
  `#ff3333` toward a deeper, more sophisticated scarlet/crimson** (around `oklch(55% 0.20 25)`)
  so it reads professional, not aggressive. Use it as the **10%** — one or two decisive moments
  per view (the wordmark accent, a key link, a section marker), never sprayed across every
  border and label. Keep the tinted-neutral OKLCH text ramp already in `index.css`.
- **Type:** This is the biggest change. **Lead with a clean, confident sans.** Monospace is
  demoted to a *small accent only* — timestamps, metadata labels, code, the occasional tag —
  never body copy or headings. Drop `font-mono` as the global default.
  - Starting pairing to validate when building (avoid the AI-reflex fonts — no Inter, Geist,
    Space Grotesk, DM Sans, etc.): a characterful editorial display such as **Bricolage
    Grotesque** or **Archivo / Archivo Expanded** for the big type, a clean readable body sans
    such as **Hanken Grotesk** or **Public Sans**, and a mono (Geist Mono / JetBrains Mono)
    reserved strictly for accents. Finalize by eye during the build.
  - Use a real modular scale with fluid `clamp()` headings; few sizes, high contrast (≥1.25).
- **Composition:** Editorial = intentional and a little asymmetric. Big confident headline,
  generous black space, left-aligned anchors, content that breathes. Not centered-everything,
  not a uniform card grid.

**Anti-references — these read as "extreme" / not us. Do NOT bring them back:**
- The shell prompt line (`root@j4ck-xyz:~$ ./home.sh --verbose`)
- `SYS_STATUS // OPERATIONAL`, `CONSOLE_LINK`, `TERMINAL_ACTIVE`, `SYSTEM_ID`, "JackOS",
  and all system/diagnostic language in the visible UI
- Monospace as the primary/body typeface
- The outlined-red `.xyz` wordmark treatment and the text-scramble / typewriter gimmicks
- The interactive in-card CLI terminal as the hero element
- Uniform dashboard tiles styled as a "system" (the bento can stay as a *layout tool*, but it
  must not look like an ops dashboard)
- Previously removed and still banned: CRT scanlines, film grain, glitch on default UI,
  cursor particle trail, Konami/vinyl easter egg.

The **devtools console easter eggs** (`jackHelp`/`neofetch`/`hire`/`glitch`/`matrix`) may stay —
they're opt-in, invisible by default, and harmless. Their playful "system" language is fine in
the console; it just must not appear in the actual page UI.

### Carries forward (don't regress these — hard-won infrastructure)
- **Measured contrast policy:** every text tier down to `--text-muted` clears WCAG AA 4.5:1;
  information-bearing text uses muted or brighter; `--text-faint` (~3.4:1) is ornament only.
- **Reduced motion:** the three-layer system (CSS media query + `MotionConfig reducedMotion="user"`
  + `useReducedMotion()`) must keep working through the redesign.
- **Performance:** lazy routes, deferred `@atproto`, split vendor chunks — keep it lean.
- **Accessibility:** real `<button>`s, visible focus, alt text, contiguous heading outline.
- **Same data & routes:** Bluesky posts, Grain/Flashes photos, Leaflet blogs, GitHub repos,
  social links — all unchanged. This is a reskin + re-layout, not a rewrite of behavior.

### Design Principles
1. **Black is the canvas, red is the gesture.** One or two decisive red moments per view; the
   rest is near-black and type. Restraint is what makes it professional.
2. **Type carries the personality, not chrome.** A confident sans, a real scale, generous space.
   No system language, no monospace walls.
3. **Editorial, not dashboard.** Intentional, slightly asymmetric composition with room to
   breathe — never a uniform grid of identical "status" tiles.
4. **It's jack's space, kept light.** First-person and personal where it helps; never expose
   personal info; never over-introduce. Handle is `@j4ck.xyz`.
5. **Sleek and fast.** Motion is subtle and purposeful; the page is quick. Keep the perf and a11y
   gains from the previous passes.
6. **Mono is seasoning.** Monospace appears only in small accents (timestamps, tags, code) — a
   nod to the maker, not the whole outfit.
